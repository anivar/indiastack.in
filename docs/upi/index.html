UPI: Claims vs Reality

The government promotes UPI as "open and interoperable." Here's what they don't tell you about who controls it and how it surveils you.

Claim #1
indiastack.global says

"UPI is an open and interoperable digital payment platform that integrates multiple bank accounts into a single mobile application."

Reality

NPCI claims to be private but takes public money. It's a consortium that controls both the standard AND the implementation, while evading accountability.

  • 51%+ owned by public sector banks. CAG appoints auditors (government company status).
  • Yet NPCI won 2019 CIC case to NOT be subject to RTI requests
  • Government gave Rs 8,730 crore (2021-2025) to subsidize RuPay and BHIM-UPI
  • Uses "National" in name despite 2014 rule limiting that to government entities
  • No alternative payment rail exists, RBI rejected all New Umbrella Entity applications
  • Source code is not published. Cannot be forked. Cannot be audited.
Not Like Email

UPI ≠ SMTP

UPI has been incorrectly compared with email and SMTP. Unlike email, UPI is not open access:

  • You cannot set up a UPI service like you can set up an email server
  • UPI protocols are not open, unlike SMTP
  • NPCI controls and gates access to the "marketplace"
  • Mozilla has criticized NODE for "openwashing" of projects

The phrase "open" for UPI has been used liberally, "open architecture", but NPCI runs and controls access to the marketplace that is UPI.

The "Open" Platform is a Duopoly

PhonePe (8.1B monthly transactions) + Google Pay (6.1B) control 85.9% of UPI. Paytm is at 6.93%. BHIM, NPCI's own app, is at just 0.2% despite NPCI giving its subsidiary Rs 300 crore to compete.

NPCI announced a 30% market share cap in 2020. The deadline has been extended repeatedly, now to December 31, 2026. The cap has never been enforced.

Claim #2
indiastack.global says

"Sovereign Solution and Multiplier of Economic activity"

Reality

UPI provides complete financial surveillance:

  • Every transaction is visible to NPCI
  • Location, merchant, amount, frequency. All tracked
  • No privacy from the payment rail operator
  • Data can be shared with government agencies
  • Cash displacement = surveillance expansion
Security Vulnerabilities
USENIX Security 2020 / Renuka Kumar et al.

Design-Level Authentication Flaws in UPI 1.0

Reverse-engineering of 7 popular UPI apps revealed multi-factor authentication design flaws that could allow a victim's bank account to be linked and emptied, even if the victim had never used a UPI app. Attacks were scalable and remote.

CVEs in BHIM (NPCI's Official UPI App)

CVE-2017-9821: Hardcoded SMS validation strings (AK-NPCIMB, IM-NPCIBM, VK-NPCIBM) enabled authentication bypass
CVE-2017-9819: OTP feature not properly restricted, easier to bypass authentication
CVE-2017-9820: Custom keypad input exposed to Accessibility service
CVE-2018-15660, CVE-2018-15661: Additional vulnerabilities reported to CERT-US
Government Audit, Feb 2019

NPCI Infrastructure Vulnerabilities

Audit revealed 40+ security vulnerabilities. Personal data including 16-digit card numbers, customer names, and account numbers were stored in plain text, unencrypted.

Claim #3
indiastack.global says

"Financial Inclusion and Cash displacement"

Reality

Financial inclusion through surveillance infrastructure:

  • Cash provides privacy, UPI eliminates it
  • Rural users often lack smartphone/internet access
  • Dependent on network connectivity, fails during outages
  • Requires bank account, excludes the unbanked
  • "Inclusion" means "inclusion in surveillance"
Differential Treatment
MediaNama / Payments Council of India / Public Statements

NPCI Has Picked Winners

NPCI has admitted to discriminating between participants:

  • Wallet companies excluded (2016): NPCI CEO AP Hota said banks asked to "leave the wallets out of it... just a competitive position."
  • Paytm delayed: Vijay Shekhar Sharma alleged NPCI asked to delay Paytm UPI launch by 4 months so Google Tez could launch first.
  • Google Tez launched with 4 banks (Axis, HDFC, ICICI, SBI), other UPI apps launched with only one partner bank.
  • WhatsApp got differential interface, NPCI called it a "beta launch for a million users."
  • WhatsApp restricted for 6 years: Limited to 1 million users (2018), then 100 million (2022), full rollout only Dec 31, 2024.
Payments Council of India (2017)

"Payments Neutrality" Demand

"Only bank-led PSPs have direct access to payment systems. Non-bank PSPs can access payment systems only through a member bank... None of these can connect to NPCI run infrastructure, and are effectively disenfranchised by the Banks that run NPCI."
