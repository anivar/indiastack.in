Can You Audit It?

Independent security audits? Not allowed. Code review? Not possible. Even government audits reveal systemic failures, but remain largely hidden.

The Pattern

Can't verify? Can't correct. Audit keeps complex systems honest. Without it, errors pile up, power concentrates, the system drifts from what it claims to do.

"Public infrastructure" that cannot be independently audited is not public. It is a black box with public funding.

What These Government Audits Are

The CAG and Parliamentary reports cited below are not independent security audits. They are government reviews of government systems, partial, years delayed, and limited by what UIDAI chose to share. RTI requests are stonewalled. Independent researchers have no access.

Yet even these constrained reviews reveal fundamental problems. Imagine what independent audits would find.

UIDAI / Aadhaar: CAG Report No. 24 of 2021
CAG Audit (2014-2019)

38% Enrolments Missing POI/POA Documents

45 crore out of 115 crore Aadhaar enrolments have missing identity/address documents. Enrolment agencies did not transfer documentation to UIDAI. "Are the people who sign up actually who they say they are? UIDAI may not know."

CAG Audit (2014-2019)

4.75 Lakh Duplicate Aadhaars Cancelled

On average, 145 duplicate Aadhaars per day were generated over 9 years since 2010 that required cancellation.

CAG Audit (2014-2019)

No Data Archiving Policy

Despite maintaining one of the world's largest biometric databases, UIDAI had no data archiving policy, a "vital storage management best practice."

CAG Audit (2014-2019)

Privacy at Risk

UIDAI had not ensured that client applications used by ecosystem partners were not capable of storing personal information. No encryption algorithm specified for Aadhaar Data Vault.

CAG Audit (2014-2019)

No Independent Security Audits

"No REs or ASAs had their operations audited annually either by a certified Information Systems Auditor or by UIDAI."

"The audit of functioning of the UIDAI is partial because of non-transparency."
- CAG Report No. 24 of 2021
World Economic Forum Global Risks Report 2019

"Largest Data Breach in the World"

"The largest [data breach] was in India, where the government ID database, Aadhaar, reportedly suffered multiple breaches that potentially compromised the records of all 1.1 billion registered citizens."

Parliamentary Standing Committee (2011-2013)

Rejected Aadhaar Three Times

  • Dec 2011: 31-member committee led by Yashwant Sinha rejected NIAI Bill on privacy, identity theft, security concerns
  • Dec 2012: Committee rejected UID project again
  • Dec 2013: Stated UIDAI was "functioning without any legal basis"

"The issue of aadhaar numbers and incurring expenditure from the exchequer by the Government is a clear circumvention of Parliament."

Function Creep
"Coercion, illegality, contempt of court and exclusion have become characteristics by which people recognise the UID project."
- Usha Ramanathan

Voluntary → Compulsory

"First Aadhaar was a voluntary exercise, then it was made compulsory." What began as optional ID for welfare became mandatory for:

  • Bank accounts (2017)
  • Mobile SIM cards (2017)
  • Tax filing / PAN (2017)
  • School admissions
  • Midday meals for children
  • Pension disbursement
  • Scholarship applications
  • HIV/AIDS treatment access

Supreme Court struck down private sector use in 2018. Government re-legislated it through amendments by 2025.

NPCI / UPI: Government Security Audit (2019)
Govt Security Audit, March 2019

40+ Security Vulnerabilities Found

Including several rated 'critical' and 'high' risk. Card numbers stored in plain text. RuPay card numbers visible in server logs. Unencrypted data in the 250,000 ATM network.

Cybersecurity Experts

Audit Quality Concerns

"Audits are done to satisfy the minimum compliance requirement and not to identify the risk exposure." Most audits awarded via lowest bidder (L1) tendering, quality compromised.

LocalCircles Survey, 2025

1 in 5 UPI Users Defrauded

Digital payment frauds (including UPI) accounted for 56.5% of all banking frauds in FY25, with losses totaling ₹520 crore.

UPI Security Research (USENIX 2020)
Renuka Kumar et al., University of Michigan

Multi-Factor Authentication Design Flaws

Reverse-engineering of 7 popular UPI apps revealed design-level flaws in UPI 1.0 that could allow a victim's bank account to be linked and emptied, even if the victim had never used a UPI app. Attacks were scalable and remote.

CVE Database

Multiple CVEs in BHIM (NPCI's Official App)

CVE-2017-9821: Hardcoded SMS validation strings (AK-NPCIMB, IM-NPCIBM, VK-NPCIBM) enabled authentication bypass.

CVE-2017-9819: OTP feature not properly restricted, easier to bypass authentication.

CVE-2017-9820: Custom keypad input exposed to Accessibility service.

CVE-2018-15660, CVE-2018-15661: Additional vulnerabilities reported to CERT-US.

ABDM / Ayushman Bharat
MediaNama, IFF (2024)

Patient Data Publicly Exposed

Names, discharge dates, and payment amounts of patients published on NHA websites, violating their own Health Data Management Policy. Up to 28 Cr beneficiary details potentially public.

FIR, Lucknow (November 2025)

Fraud: 300+ Fake Ayushman Cards

Aadhaar-linked mobile numbers changed without knowledge. Fraudsters accessed government health portals using unauthorized OTPs. Union Health Ministry ordered internal audit.

ONDC / Account Aggregator

Cannot Be Audited by Public

  • ONDC: Section 8 company. RTI Act exempt. Cannot file writ petition for rights violations.
  • Account Aggregator: Self-regulated by Sahamati (private non-profit). Biennial audits are self-administered.
  • NPCI: Similar "not for profit" structure. No public accountability mechanism.
What "Auditable" Should Mean

True Public Infrastructure

Open source code. Public audit logs. Independent security research welcome. Bug bounty programs. RTI-accessible. Parliamentary oversight. Judicial review.

"India Stack" Is PR Branding

There is no single "India Stack." It is a marketing term for projects owned and controlled by different entities:

  • Aadhaar: UIDAI (government trust), RTI-exempt, CAG found 38% missing documents, no independent audit access
  • UPI: NPCI (private bank consortium), 2019 govt audit found 40+ vulnerabilities, code not public
  • Account Aggregator: ReBIT spec, Sahamati self-regulation, private oversight of private implementation
  • ONDC: Section 8 company, RTI-exempt, cannot file writ petition
  • ABDM: NHA, patient data exposed on public dashboards, violating own policy

None of these allow independent security audits. Even Aadhaar, the oldest component, has never been properly audited. CAG admitted their review was "partial because of non-transparency." Parliamentary Standing Committee called it "circumvention of Parliament." RTI queries are stonewalled.

"Aadhaar is defective by design. By the time they understand it, many will die, many others will be excluded, and still others will be victims of identity fraud. Finally, they will understand and Aadhaar will be dead. But it is a long process. What we can do now is improve the speed of this learning process."
- Anivar Aravind, Times of India (September 2018)

Systems Without Error Correction

Healthy systems have feedback loops. When something fails, the failure is detected, reported, and corrected. India Stack has no such mechanism.

  • When biometric authentication fails, the system does not record why, it just denies service
  • When citizens die due to welfare exclusion, there is no automatic correction
  • When data is breached, there is no public disclosure requirement
  • When the system makes errors, the citizen must prove the error, the system assumes it is right

A system that cannot detect its own failures will amplify them.

Verdict
No Independent Audit Allowed. Government's Own Audits Reveal Failure.
