The Architecture

Financial records flow through Account Aggregator. Documents through DigiLocker. Your face through Digi Yatra. Each promises convenience. Each enables surveillance. Together, they see everything.

DigiLocker Document wallet → OTP vulnerabilities → SIM swap risks

Critical Vulnerability: SIM Card Exposure

A surrendered SIM card, reassigned to a new user by a telecom provider, was used to open a new DigiLocker account. The Aadhaar details of the previous SIM card holder were displayed in the newly-created account.

This violates DPDP Act and demonstrates inability to delink Aadhaar from DigiLocker once associated.

  • OTP Authentication Weakness: RBI has questioned OTP-based authentication for financial transactions. DigiLocker still relies on it.
  • Cannot Delink Aadhaar: Once Aadhaar is linked to DigiLocker, there's no way to remove the association.
  • SIM Cloning Risk: SIM card cloning, swapping, or blocking can compromise DigiLocker access.
  • Fake DigiLocker Apps: Ministry of Electronics issued warnings about counterfeit apps harvesting identity data.
  • Cloud Storage Concerns: Data stored in cloud, concerns about unauthorized access and service disruptions.
  • 248 Bank Breaches: Between June 2018 and March 2022, Indian banks recorded 248 successful data breaches.

IFF Position: Complete Withdrawal

IFF urges officials "to completely withdraw Digi Yatra from Indian airports owing to its large gamut of concerns relating to privacy, surveillance, exclusion errors and lack of institutional accountability and transparency."

  • Coercive Enrollment: Survey found ~30% of enrolled passengers signed up without knowing, airport staff captured face biometrics without consent.
  • "Tool of Coercion": IFF states Digi Yatra has "become a tool of coercion and deception for airport staff against unsuspecting passengers."
  • Facial Recognition Dangers: IFF warns FRT-based systems enable "creation of 360-degree profiles of citizens" and "dragnet surveillance."
  • Legal Battle: Digi Yatra Foundation vs. ex-tech partner Data Evolve, dispute over ownership of millions of users' personal data.
  • No Central Storage Claim: Government claims no central storage, but airport operators control data. Regulated by private foundation, not ministry.
  • International Expansion: Pilot for foreign nationals planned for June 2025, extending FRT surveillance to international travelers.
  • 24-Hour Deletion Claim: Ministry says data deleted within 24 hours of departure, no independent verification possible.

The Pattern: Consent as Theater

"Facial data stored from FRT-based authentication systems is far more vulnerable than any other biometric identifier as it can facilitate creation of 360-degree profiles of citizens and can result in 'dragnet surveillance.'"
- Internet Freedom Foundation

Consent is a checkbox. You check it or you don't participate. That's not consent, it's an ultimatum.

  • Manufactured Consent: Terms nobody reads. Single checkbox for everything. Staff who enroll you without asking.
  • No Exit: Want a loan? Share your data. Want to board faster? Share your face. The "choice" is participate or be excluded.
  • Aggregation = Power: Each "convenience" is an aggregation point. Combined, they see everything.
  • Private Foundation Control: Digi Yatra Foundation (private entity) controls biometric data. Not subject to RTI.
  • Trust Us: Encrypted? Prove it. Deleted? Prove it. Code is closed. You cannot verify anything.
