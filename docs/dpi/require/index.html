How to Require Corrigibility

Making DPI constitutionally enforceable.

Minimum structural conditions.

Corrigibility cannot be left to policy intent, good faith, or post-hoc review.

If Digital Public Infrastructure exercises population-scale power, corrigibility must be mandated ex ante through law, procurement, and regulatory conditions.

This page provides illustrative examples of how the five corrigibility tests can be translated into binding requirements.

Illustrative Instruments

Corrigibility is a structural property. Different legal and institutional contexts may enforce it in different ways.

The following clauses, requirements, and language are provided as illustrative examples of how corrigibility surfaces can be made inspectable in procurement or designation processes. Their use is optional.

Inclusion, modification, or omission is a policy decision for the adopting institution. The framework evaluates structure, not instruments.

These requirements are not aspirational.
They are minimum structural conditions.

The Corrigibility Frame

Corrigibility is not a value preference. It is a structural property that determines whether power exercised through systems remains governable.

A system is corrigible only if those affected by its operation can:

detect

error

contest

decisions

trigger

correction

exit

without penalty

enable

replacement

Where these capacities are absent, power becomes unidirectional and self-stabilizing.

Law must therefore require corrigibility as a condition of public infrastructure.

Conceptual Foundations

The requirements below rest on three independent traditions. Each arrives at the same conclusion through a different path.

CYBERNETICS

Feedback and Control

In control theory, a regulator must possess sufficient feedback to correct error in the system it governs. Where feedback is blocked, systems drift toward failure regardless of intent.

Legal implication: Systems exercising public power must be structurally open to feedback from those they affect.

COMMONS

Ostrom's Conditions

Commons are sustainable only when users can participate in rule-making, monitor behavior, resolve disputes, and prevent enclosure.

Legal implication: DPI is a shared resource. Without structural safeguards, it becomes enclosed and governed by operators rather than users.

FREE SOFTWARE

Inspectability and Forkability

FOSS emerged from the recognition that software governing others must be inspectable and replaceable. The possibility of a fork disciplines power even when unused.

Legal implication: Public infrastructure that cannot be inspected or replaced cannot be held accountable.

Corrigibility is the point at which these three traditions converge.

The Constitutional Foundation

India's Right to Privacy judgment (2017), a globally significant constitutional ruling, establishes that any state system affecting autonomy, dignity, or access to essential services must satisfy proportionality: legality, necessity, and non-excessive harm.

However, proportionality presumes that systems are:

Legible in operation
Auditable in practice
Reversible in effect
Alternatives exist

Where digital systems block exit, hide execution, prevent independent audit, or cannot be replaced, proportionality cannot function. Judicial review collapses into deference.

Corrigibility supplies the structural preconditions that constitutional doctrines like proportionality presuppose but cannot themselves enforce.

CONSTITUTIONAL LINKAGE

The Right to Privacy judgment (Puttaswamy v. Union of India, 2017) requires proportionality: legality, necessity, and minimal impairment. These judicial preconditions presuppose legibility, reversibility, alternatives, and independent review. The five corrigibility tests operationalize those preconditions into a testable evidence set (see Exposure Surfaces).

ENFORCEMENT GUIDANCE

DPI designation should be conditional on demonstrable evidence for each corrigibility test.

The Corrigibility Exposure Surfaces defines the minimal artifacts evaluators should require.

→ Partial evidence is suitable only for limited pilots with mandated fallback measures

→ Full evidence should be required for DPI designation

× No national security, efficiency, or innovation exception

REQUIREMENT 01

Statutory Right to Safe Refusal

EXIT

No person shall be denied access to government services, welfare benefits, legal identity, or civic participation for refusing or discontinuing digital participation.

Equivalent non-digital pathways must remain available, functional, and non-punitive.

CONCEPTUAL BASIS

Cybernetics: Exit is a primary feedback signal. Without it, systems cannot register failure.

Commons: Participation must be voluntary to remain legitimate.

Constitutional law: Consent without refusal is coercive.

APPLICATION TO LEARNED SYSTEMS

Where learned systems mediate services invisibly, disclosure is mandatory. Non-AI alternatives must remain available. Refusal of AI mediation must not result in denial of service.

REQUIREMENT 02

Reproducible Execution as Condition of Use

CODE

The complete executing source code of any DPI system shall be:

  • publicly available
  • reproducible from source
  • licensed to permit inspection, modification, and reuse

API documentation, standards compliance, or policy descriptions do not satisfy this requirement.

CONCEPTUAL BASIS

FOSS: Inspectability is a precondition of trust.

Cybernetics: Feedback requires visibility into control logic.

Constitutional law: Opaque execution functions as secret law.

APPLICATION TO LEARNED SYSTEMS

Behavior is determined by training, not inference alone. Training data, training processes, and alignment decisions must be disclosed. Weights without training context replicate the problem of open specifications without open execution.

REQUIREMENT 03

Independent Audit Without Permission

AUDIT

Independent parties must be able to:

  • inspect system behavior
  • measure error and exclusion rates
  • test security and integrity
  • publish findings

without operator approval, contractual restriction, or legal threat.

CONCEPTUAL BASIS

Cybernetics: Error cannot be corrected if it cannot be measured.

Commons: Monitoring must be accountable to users, not operators.

Constitutional law: Proportionality requires evidence, not assertion.

APPLICATION TO LEARNED SYSTEMS

Benchmarks are insufficient. Sustained behavioral verification in deployment conditions is required. Model updates during audit periods must be disclosed.

REQUIREMENT 04

Constitutive Governance Authority

GOVERN

Affected populations must have binding authority in:

  • system design decisions
  • eligibility rules
  • access criteria
  • future modifications

Consultation, feedback portals, or grievance redress mechanisms do not constitute governance.

CONCEPTUAL BASIS

Commons: Rules must be made by those bound by them.

Cybernetics: Governance is a feedback channel, not a complaint sink.

Constitutional law: Legitimate aims cannot be unilaterally asserted.

APPLICATION TO LEARNED SYSTEMS

Training objectives and optimization targets are governance decisions. Affected populations must participate in defining what systems are designed to optimize.

REQUIREMENT 05

Perpetual Forkability and Replaceability

FORK

DPI systems must be legally and technically forkable:

  • alternative implementations must be permitted
  • deployment without incumbent permission must be lawful
  • re-enclosure through contracts, IP, or regulation is prohibited

CONCEPTUAL BASIS

FOSS: Forkability disciplines power.

Cybernetics: Replacement is the ultimate corrective action.

Constitutional law: Necessity requires less restrictive alternatives.

APPLICATION TO LEARNED SYSTEMS

Licensing alone is insufficient. Concentration of compute, data, or training resources can function as effective barriers. Forkability must be practical, not theoretical.

Compliance and Review

Designation as Digital Public Infrastructure shall be conditional and revocable.

Failure to maintain corrigibility requirements at any point automatically suspends DPI status, regardless of past approval.

Corrigibility is not a one-time certification. It is a continuous structural obligation.

Why Law Must Require Corrigibility

Markets do not select for corrigibility. Institutions optimize for rollout speed, scale, and control.

Without legal mandate:

→ exit paths are closed

→ audits are restricted

→ governance is internalized

→ alternatives are prohibited

Correction does not disappear, it reroutes through courts, protests, media exposure, and political crisis.

Requiring corrigibility shifts correction upstream, reducing harm and litigation.

Corrigibility is the minimum structural condition under which digital systems exercising public power can remain constitutional, stable, and legitimate.

This holds across technologies, including deterministic systems and learned systems.

If a system cannot be corrected by those it affects, it must not be deployed as public infrastructure.
