Aadhaar: Claims vs Reality

The government promotes Aadhaar as "open architecture" with "strong security." Here's what they don't tell you.

Is source code published? No. Zero lines.
Independent security audit? Never conducted.
RTI queries answered? Exempt since 2016.
Can you delete your data? No mechanism exists.
THE FOUNDATION

Aadhaar is the master key to India Stack. Every other layer, UPI, ABDM, DigiLocker, Account Aggregator. Authenticates through Aadhaar. When the foundation is closed, RTI-exempt, and unauditable, everything built on it inherits those properties.

CLAIM #1: OPEN ARCHITECTURE

indiastack.global says

"Entire technology architecture behind Aadhaar is based on principles of openness, linear scalability, strong security, and most importantly vendor neutrality."

Reality

No source code has ever been published. The claim of "openness" refers to API specifications, not the actual system.

  • UIDAI is RTI-exempt under Section 24 of RTI Act since 2016
  • No independent security audit has ever been conducted
  • Cannot be forked or independently deployed
  • "GitHub / OpenForge Link" on indiastack.global: No actual link exists

CLAIM #2: DATA SECURITY

indiastack.global says

"Data Security", listed as a core principle of Aadhaar's technology backbone.

Reality

The World Economic Forum called Aadhaar data exposure "potentially the largest data breach in history."

The Tribune, January 2018

Rs 500 Buys Access to Billion Aadhaar Details

Reporter purchased login credentials from agents, accessed names, phone numbers, addresses, photographs, and bank details of any Aadhaar holder. The entire CIDR database was accessible for a trivial sum.

Kaarana / Security Researcher

ECMP Software Cracked for Rs 2,500

Aadhaar enrolment software available on the black market. Used to create fake Aadhaar numbers and enrol people fraudulently. The software patch bypassed all security controls.

UIDAI Admission

210 Government Websites Exposed Data

UIDAI itself admitted that 210 government websites had publicly displayed Aadhaar numbers and personal details, violating their own data protection claims.

Who Aadhaar Was Built For

  • Office workers with soft hands
  • Urban populations with connectivity
  • Young adults with clear biometrics
  • Those with stable addresses

Who Gets Excluded

  • Manual laborers (worn fingerprints)
  • Elderly (fading biometrics)
  • Disabled persons
  • Those without stable mobile numbers

The Paradox: The very populations that welfare schemes are meant to serve, laborers, elderly, disabled. Are systematically excluded by the biometric authentication that's supposed to help them.

Parliamentary Standing Committee, 2025

MPs Warn of Systematic Exclusion

Across party lines, MPs warned that faulty fingerprint and iris scans are blocking recipients from PDS rations and MGNREGA work. Laborers' worn fingerprints and elderly citizens' changed iris patterns no longer match UIDAI records. Millions denied due to technical errors.

SYSTEM FAILURES

Authentication Bottleneck

Aadhaar is the single point of failure for all India Stack services. When UIDAI servers fail, everything stops:

7,000+

Connectivity Dependency: Rural areas with poor internet cannot authenticate. No non-digital fallback exists. When the system fails, citizens have no alternative government pathway.

CHILDREN ENROLLED WITHOUT CONSENT

Supreme Court Puttaswamy (2018)

Promise: Exit at 18

The Supreme Court ruled that children enrolled by parents "shall have the option to exit upon attaining majority."

UIDAI to Supreme Court (2018)

Reality: No Exit Implemented

UIDAI told the Supreme Court that minors cannot opt out of Aadhaar after turning 18, directly contradicting the court's direction. The exit mechanism was never implemented.

2.5+ crore children enrolled without understanding the system. No procedural safeguards for consent. No review of information. No deletion mechanism when they become adults. Once enrolled, always enrolled. Biometric data collected as a child remains in CIDR forever.

CLAIM #5: LEGAL FRAMEWORK

indiastack.global says

"Aadhaar Act 2016 provides legal backing". UIDAI is a "statutory authority"

Reality

The legal framework was designed to prevent accountability:

  • Aadhaar Act passed as Money Bill to bypass Rajya Sabha objections
  • Parliamentary Standing Committee rejected the bill three times
  • UIDAI declared RTI-exempt organization
  • No mechanism for deletion of Aadhaar data
  • Linked to NATGRID surveillance system

CLAIM #6: VENDOR NEUTRALITY

indiastack.global says

"Vendor neutrality", suggesting open competition and choice

Reality

There is only one implementation. You cannot:

  • Run your own Aadhaar-compatible identity system
  • Choose an alternative provider
  • Opt out once enrolled
  • Verify how authentication decisions are made

Vendor neutrality without source code is meaningless. True vendor neutrality requires that anyone can implement the system. With Aadhaar, you can only call UIDAI's API, you cannot verify it, audit it, or build alternatives.
