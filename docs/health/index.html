Health Stack

Digital health infrastructure built on consent theater, closed code, and surveillance capability

"40% of the current 328 million ABHA IDs originated from CoWIN. In many cases, these IDs were automatically generated without any information or consent from the user when people used their Aadhaar for booking COVID-19 vaccinations."
- SFLC.in Research

The Architecture

India's Health Stack comprises interconnected systems that collect, store, and share health data of over a billion citizens. Built during and after the COVID-19 pandemic under emergency conditions, these systems were deployed without adequate privacy frameworks, parliamentary oversight, or independent audits.

CoWIN Vaccination platform → ABHA generator → Health surveillance
Aarogya Setu Contact tracing → Health passport → Location surveillance
ABDM/ABHA Health ID → Unified health record → Lifelong medical profile
eSanjeevani Telemedicine → Health data collection → Centralized records
UHID Unique Health ID → Hospital linkage → Lifelong patient tracking

CoWIN Closed Source

Government Claims

Secure vaccination management platform. Database is "safe and secure." Privacy-preserving design.

Documented Reality

No specific privacy policy. No information on developer or cost. Data breaches reported in June 2021 and January 2022.

  • No Privacy Policy: SFLC.in found CoWIN app has no specific privacy policy. No official email address listed.
  • Facial Recognition: IFF confirmed through RTI that government uses facial recognition for vaccine beneficiary verification.
  • Automatic ABHA Creation: Health IDs created without user consent during vaccination booking using Aadhaar.
  • Data Breach Claims: June 2021 reports claimed 150 million Indians' data exposed. January 2022 similar reports.
  • API Data Exposure: Updated API allows government and private entities to track vaccination status of individuals.
  • Opacity: "A lot of opacity behind who developed the app, its security implications, data sharing, data retention", SFLC.in

Aarogya Setu Partial Source

Government Claims

Open source contact tracing app. Voluntary adoption. Data stored on user device. Published source code.

Documented Reality

Server-side code never released. Made mandatory for workers. Uses static Digital ID traceable to user. MIT gave 1 out of 5 stars.

  • Not Truly Open Source: Four months after release, only partial client code available. Server-side code never published.
  • Static Digital ID: Unlike Singapore's app, Aarogya Setu uses static DiD that can be retraced to user permanently.
  • GPS + Bluetooth: Collects both GPS location and Bluetooth data. GPS collection considered unnecessary globally.
  • Mandatory Adoption: Made mandatory for government and private employees despite no legal backing.
  • "Utterly Illegal": Former Supreme Court Justice B.N. Srikrishna called the mandate "utterly illegal."
  • Security Flaws: French researcher showed vulnerabilities allowing hackers to know who is infected in any area including PM's office.
  • MIT Rating: 1 out of 5 stars for collecting more data than necessary.
  • Data Repurposing: Being repurposed beyond COVID contact tracing. SFLC warns against purpose limitation violations.

ABDM / ABHA Closed Source

Government Claims

Federated architecture. Privacy by design. No centralized repository. Patient consent required. Voluntary enrollment.

Documented Reality

Mass enrollment through CoWIN without consent. 1 in 2 Indians now have ABHA. Dashboard exposed. No independent security audit.

  • Consent Violation: 40% of 328 million ABHA IDs auto-created via CoWIN without user knowledge or consent.
  • Dashboard Exposure: MediaNama reported sensitive ABDM dashboard data exposure.
  • Star Health Breach: September 2024: 31 million Indians' health insurance data leaked on Telegram.
  • Samsung Integration: ABHA integrated into Samsung Galaxy phones, expanding attack surface.
  • No Data Protection: DPDP Rules 2025 have no special protections for health data.
  • Coercive Enrollment: "Voluntary on paper but mandatory in practice" during pandemic vulnerability.
  • Regulatory Gaps: Health Data Management Policy drafted but NHA habitually displays personal patient data.

eSanjeevani Closed Source

Government Claims

Secure telemedicine platform. End-to-end encryption. 150 million consultations. Bridging healthcare gaps.

Documented Reality

No independent security audit. Code not available. Data retention policies unclear. Integrated with ABHA.

  • No Independent Audit: Claims of security not verified by third-party auditors.
  • Closed Source: Cannot verify encryption or security claims.
  • Data Integration: Connected to ABDM ecosystem, all consultations become part of lifelong health record.
  • Rural Digital Divide: Claimed to serve rural areas but requires smartphone, internet, and digital literacy.

UHID Closed Source

Government Claims

Unique identifier for seamless healthcare. Interoperability across hospitals. Patient convenience. Reduced duplication.

Documented Reality

Permanent patient tracking across all healthcare facilities. Linked to Aadhaar. No deletion mechanism. Centralizes medical history.

  • Permanent Identifier: Once assigned, UHID follows patient for life across all hospitals and clinics.
  • Aadhaar Linkage: UHID linked to Aadhaar, enabling cross-referencing with financial, telecom, and other databases.
  • No Opt-Out: Patients cannot refuse UHID assignment at registered healthcare facilities.
  • Central Repository: Despite "federated" claims, creates centralized view of patient across healthcare system.
  • Data Aggregation: Enables building comprehensive medical profiles from fragmented hospital visits.
  • Insurance Integration: Health insurance companies can access UHID-linked records for risk profiling.

The Pattern: Health as Enclosure

"The data collected through Aarogya Setu and CoWIN should not be used for any other purposes, as such use would be against the principle of purpose limitation. The absence of a data protection law should not be an excuse to conduct such exercises affecting the rights of citizens."
- Prasanth Sugathan, Legal Director, SFLC.in

Your health data flows one direction: from you to them. You generate it. They aggregate it. You see nothing back.

  • Emergency Deployment: Built in crisis. Deployed without scrutiny. Never audited. Still running.
  • Consent Theater: "Voluntary" until you need a vaccine. Then mandatory. The word means nothing.
  • Function Creep: COVID was the emergency. Surveillance is permanent. The temporary became forever.
  • Closed Code: They claim "open." The code is closed. You cannot audit. You must trust.
  • One-Way Flow: You generate data. They collect it. You see nothing back.
