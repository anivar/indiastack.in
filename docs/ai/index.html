AI Stack

Code first. Governance later. AI follows the India Stack pattern.

PRware Gets an AI Layer

"India Stack" is PRware, a marketing term with no single owner. Now AI becomes the next layer of PRware. Private entities design and build. Government provides compute and endorsement. Integration with existing closed systems creates adoption. Governance frameworks arrive after deployment.

The same sequence. The same accountability gaps. Now with algorithmic decision-making layered on top of closed, unauditable infrastructure.

The Sequence

AI follows the same sequence as the rest of India Stack:

1
Code
Private startups build models. Research labs design architectures. Specifications drafted by industry. Government provides compute access. No parliamentary approval for what gets built.
↓
2
Deployment
Integration with Bhashini, UIDAI, ONDC, ABDM. AI capabilities embedded in existing stack layers. Early adopters in government services. Ecosystem forms around deployed models.
↓
3
Adoption Push
Government services told to integrate AI. Existing stack touchpoints become AI distribution channels. Network effects through mandatory infrastructure. Opting out means opting out of the stack.
↓
4
Endorsement
"Sovereign AI" narratives. International showcases. Success metrics announced. The AI is now national identity.
↓
5
Governance
Frameworks drafted after deployment. AIGG, TPEC, AISI coordinate. 13+ month cycles for any decision. By the time governance catches up, the models are already in production.
Same order. Code first. Governance last. By the time there's a framework, there's already an ecosystem, integrations, and millions of users.

Stack Integration

AI Embedded in Existing Layers

AI isn't a separate layer. It's being integrated into existing India Stack infrastructure:

  • Bhashini: AI-powered translation. Integrated with government services.
  • UIDAI: AI for identity verification. Biometric matching at scale.
  • ONDC: AI for commerce. Recommendations, logistics.
  • ABDM: AI for health. Built on digital health records.
Each integration inherits the governance model of the underlying layer. Same accountability gaps. Same audit limitations. Now with AI decision-making added.

Governance Latency

The Decision Pipeline

AIGG (coordinates) → TPEC (advises) → AISI (implements) →
Sectoral Regulators (enforce) → Industry (complies)

LATENCY:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
AIGG meets quarterly           = 3-month cycles
TPEC reviews               = +1 month
AISI drafts standards          = +6 months
Sectoral interpretation         = +3 months
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
TOTAL: 13+ months per governance decision

MODEL RELEASE CYCLES: 12-18 months
GOVERNANCE CYCLES: 13+ months

By the time governance catches up, the models have moved on.

The Consensus Requirement

  • 6+ ministries with representation
  • 5+ sectoral regulators
  • Multiple advisory bodies
  • Inter-departmental coordination
System speed limited by slowest component. One node can slow the entire pipeline. Models deploy faster than governance can adapt.

The Seven Sutras

When governance finally arrives, it arrives empty. India's AI Governance Guidelines (2025) offer seven principles, and zero tests.

Principles Without Verification

SUTRA              SOUNDS LIKE     WOULD REQUIRE
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Trust as Foundation       Legitimacy     EXIT (can refuse)
People First          Rights       GOVERN (binding input)
Fairness & Equity        Inclusion      AUDIT (verification)
Accountability         Oversight      CODE + AUDIT
Understandable by Design    Transparency    CODE (legible execution)
Safety / Resilience       Protection     AUDIT + persistence
Innovation over Restraint   ,          deprioritizes enforcement
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
RESOURCE ALLOCATION:
₹10,371 crore for compute
38,000 GPUs subsidized
Governance: voluntary, existing laws, no new enforcement
Same pattern as G20 DPI definition. Aspirational vocabulary. No structural requirements. "Innovation over Restraint" is the only operational principle, the other six are decoration.

The Accountability Gap

When AI Decisions Fail

WHEN AI FAILS              WHO'S ACCOUNTABLE?
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
AI denies benefits            Not the model builder, "following specs"
→ citizen excluded            Not the integrator, "model output"
                     Not the government, "private technology"
                     → Citizen has no recourse

AI flags for fraud            Not the algorithm, "probabilistic"
→ account frozen             Not the bank, "AI recommendation"
                     Not the regulator, "implementation detail"
                     → User has no recourse

AI makes health recommendation      Not the model, "advisory only"
→ wrong treatment            Not the hospital, "AI-assisted"
                     Not the platform, "user responsibility"
                     → Patient has no recourse

THE PATTERN:
Same as rest of India Stack.
Responsibility distributed until it disappears.
Everyone points elsewhere.
Citizens bear the consequences.
