Human Rights Watch Findings

India: Identification Project Threatens Rights (2018)

Human Rights Watch and Amnesty International India joint finding:

2.5 million families in Rajasthan denied ration supplies between September 2016 and June 2017 due to missing or unlinked Aadhaar numbers.

Children without Aadhaar excluded from government schools and free meal programs, despite constitutional guarantees.

Hospitals demanded Aadhaar enrollment before issuing birth certificates. Death certificates similarly conditioned.

People living with HIV/AIDS denied medical treatment over Aadhaar requirements.

Aadhaar deactivations occur without prior notice. Only UIDAI can sue, individuals cannot.

Why Authentication Fails
"Around two to five per cent of the Indian population would be excluded" due to biometric failures. Physical conditions like corneal blindness, scars, and malnourishment-induced cataracts prevented valid fingerprints or iris scans.
- Usha Ramanathan, CIS India

Biometric Exclusion

Fingerprints wear away from manual labor. Construction workers, farmers, domestic workers face 6-12% authentication failure rates.

Elderly people have degraded fingerprints. Children's biometrics are unstable. People with disabilities face accessibility barriers.

In 2023, UIDAI acknowledged 54+ hours of service downtime due to OTP delays and server issues.

2023-25: Facial Authentication Expansion

Despite Known Failures, Biometrics Expand

After years of documented fingerprint exclusion, states are now adding facial authentication. Rajasthan mandated it for pensioners via RAJSSP app (February 2023). Results from January-April 2025:

Facial authentication success: 69.11%, nearly 1 in 3 pensioners fail.

Fingerprint authentication: 76.27% success. OTP had 90%+ success but government deemed it "unreliable."

The system was tested on only 15-20 people before statewide rollout to 600,000+ pensioners in Jaipur alone.

Consent Is Structurally Broken

UIDAI claims authentication requires "explicit consent." But consent is meaningless when refusal means exclusion.

This pattern repeats across India Stack:

  • Aadhaar: No ration card without it. No pension. No bank account.
  • UPI: Bank account required. KYC mandatory. Phone number linked.
  • ABDM: Health ID pushed at hospitals. Opt-out unclear.
  • FASTag: Mandatory by law. No alternative.
  • CoWIN: No vaccine without registration. Digital-only access.

When the cost of refusal is starvation, medical exclusion, or losing your pension, that's not consent. That's coercion.

"No match." The system's response when a 92-year-old's face doesn't match its database. No pension. No recourse. No accountability.
- The Wire (2025)
Digital Divide: CoWIN

Vaccine Access as Digital Gatekeeping

CoWIN required smartphone, internet, and digital literacy. 200-280 million Indians (33-45% of 18-44 age group) had no or poor internet access.

25% of India's population had no smartphone. No offline registration option. English-only web portal. Homeless and rural populations systematically excluded.

Supreme Court (May 2021): "CoWIN not accessible to persons with disabilities." Ordered disability audit.

Digital divide privileged English-speaking urban population at the cost of rural India. A form of "technical rationing."

The Research

Key Findings

  • Documented (2019): Of 42 hunger-related deaths since 2017, 25 were linked to Aadhaar issues.
  • Washington Post (2018): "India's biometric ID program was supposed to end welfare corruption. But the neediest may be hit hardest." Authentication failures prevented thousands from receiving entitlements.
  • JPAL Study (2020): 88% of 1.44 lakh cancelled ration cards in Jharkhand belonged to genuine beneficiaries, not "ghosts."
  • IIT Delhi (Reetika Khera): Continued quantity fraud, higher transaction costs, and exclusion of the most vulnerable since Aadhaar-Based Biometric Authentication.
  • Right to Food Campaign: 57 starvation deaths documented in nine Indian states from 2015 onwards. At least 19 directly linked to Aadhaar.
  • CAG Report (2022): UIDAI takes no responsibility for deficient biometric capture. Onus passed to Aadhaar holders.
  • SFLC.in (2021): CoWIN created digital exclusion, mandatory for 18-44 age group, disadvantaging those without smartphones or internet.
"Far from being inclusive and reducing corruption, Aadhaar is becoming a tool of exclusion. Whatever is termed as savings is often the result of a denial of legal entitlements."
- Reetika Khera, IIT Delhi
"When subsidies are subject to biometric authentication, uninterrupted network connectivity, and bureaucratic compliance, the right to food becomes a conditional privilege."

The Court Response

Orissa High Court (May 2023)

"Welfare schemes are meant to cater to the needs of the most vulnerable and poor sections of our society who cannot be excluded on any ground, including the lack of an Aadhaar Card or a mobile phone."

The court noted that Subhalaxmi Tarai, suffering from cerebral palsy and secondary malnutrition, had died.

Supreme Court (March 2021)

Agreed to hear petition challenging deletion of 3 crore ration cards. The petition (Koili Devi v. Union of India) was filed by Santoshi Kumari's mother.

Starvation deaths documented across: Jharkhand, UP, Odisha, Karnataka, MP, Maharashtra, Bihar, Chhattisgarh, West Bengal, Andhra Pradesh, Telangana.

Ongoing Exclusion

Section 57 Restored (TechCrunch, 2025)

In 2018, Supreme Court struck down Section 57 that allowed private entities to use Aadhaar. The government has now effectively re-legislated this through amendments.

Private companies in e-commerce, hospitality, healthcare can now access Aadhaar authentication. No guardrails defined to prevent misuse of biometric IDs.

e-KYC Drive (2024)

June 2024: Government directed 100% e-KYC compliance for all ration card holders.

Odisha "temporarily suspended" rice distribution for 20+ lakh eligible individuals for e-KYC non-compliance.

The Right to Food Campaign has urged the government to halt e-KYC and remove exclusionary digital measures from PDS.

When Systems Fail, Humans Pay

Every system has errors. The question is: who bears the cost?

In India Stack, the system never fails. It only denies. When your biometrics don't match, when seeding is wrong, when the network is down, you failed. Not the system. You must prove it wrong. You cannot opt out. You cannot see why you were denied. You cannot fix it yourself. You can only wait, appeal, and hope.

With 6-12% biometric failure rates, millions are denied. The system records no error. For some, that denial is fatal.

VERDICT
Errors Are Externalized. Accountability Is Not.
